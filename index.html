Dr. Muhammad Yunus, a visionary economist, social entrepreneur, and Nobel Peace Prize laureate. Born in 1940 in Chittagong, Bangladesh, Dr. Yunus is renowned for his pioneering work in poverty alleviation through microfinance and social business. In 1976, he founded the Grameen Bank, a groundbreaking institution that provides small loans to the poor, especially women, without requiring collateral. His idea was simple yet revolutionary: empower people who are excluded from traditional financial systems by giving them access to credit. This innovative approach has lifted millions of people out of poverty, not only in Bangladesh but around the world. Dr. Yunus's vision goes beyond banking. He has established numerous social businesses aimed at addressing critical global issues, including healthcare, education, and environmental sustainability. His work demonstrates that businesses can thrive while serving a social purpose. In 2006, he and the Grameen Bank were jointly awarded the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to create economic and social development from below. Dr. Yunus continues to inspire generations to believe in a world where poverty can be eradicated.
